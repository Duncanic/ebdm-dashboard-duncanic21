Problem Focus: Employee blowout

Short description:
Employee blowout refers to frequent episodes of burnout, sudden resignations, and abrupt declines in productivity among staff caused by chronic workload imbalance, unclear role expectations, and insufficient support.

PICOC summary:
P (Population): Frontline employees and middle managers in the operations and customer-support teams (approx. 120 staff).
I (Intervention): Introduce workload rebalancing, clarify role responsibilities, implement manager training on psychological safety, and offer targeted well-being support (coaching + flexible scheduling trial).
C (Comparison): Current ad-hoc support and reactive HR interventions (status quo) or a limited wellness stipend-only approach.
O (Outcomes): Reduced voluntary turnover rate (target: -20% in 12 months), improved engagement scores (+15 points on pulse surveys), decreased sick-days (-25%), and improved time-to-resolution metrics.
C (Context): Mid-sized technology firm operating across two locations; hybrid work model; high growth over past 18 months.

Clear problem statement:
Employees in operations and customer-support are experiencing frequent burnout and abrupt departures that disrupt service delivery and increase costs; a structured, evidence-based workload and manager-support intervention is required to stabilize retention and performance.

Why this matters:
- Impact on organization: Increased recruitment costs, knowledge loss, and inconsistent customer experience.
- Stakeholder impact: Customers face longer resolution times; remaining staff experience higher workloads and decreased morale.
- Strategic alignment: Stabilizing retention supports product quality, customer retention, and cost control.

Evidence questions to explore:
- For the problem: What do exit interviews, sick-day records, and engagement surveys reveal about root causes and prevalence?
- For the solution: Which workload-rebalancing practices and manager-support programs have measurable success in comparable firms?

Next steps:
1) Collect HR metrics (turnover, sick days, engagement) for the last 18 months.
2) Pilot manager training and flexible scheduling in one team for 3 months and measure outcomes.
