Table of core search terms, short definitions, why each matters, and example search phrases.

Keyword Definition Why it matters Example search terms
Burnout Workplace syndrome of chronic job stress leading to exhaustion, cynicism, and reduced efficacy. Primary outcome for many studies; core target for interventions. "burnout" OR "occupational burnout"
Emotional exhaustion Feeling drained of emotional resources due to work demands. Often the leading subscale in burnout measures (e.g., MBI). "emotional exhaustion"
Depersonalization / Cynicism Developing a detached, impersonal attitude toward recipients or colleagues. Indicates reduced engagement and can affect care quality. "depersonalization" OR "cynicism"
Reduced personal accomplishment Perceived decline in competence or achievement at work. Relates to performance and retention outcomes. "reduced personal accomplishment"
Occupational / Job stress Stressors originating from job demands, role conflict, or organizational factors. Common exposure linked to burnout; target for organizational interventions. "occupational stress" OR "job stress"
Job strain High demands combined with low control (Karasek model). Used in causal models of burnout risk. "job strain" OR "job demand control"
Workload Volume or intensity of tasks required of staff. Modifiable factor; often targeted in interventions. "workload" OR "workload intensity"
Job control Degree of autonomy and decision latitude at work. Low control is a known moderator of stress effects. "job control" OR "decision latitude"
Social support Emotional or practical support from colleagues and supervisors. Protective factor; a common intervention target. "social support" AND (work OR workplace)
Compassion fatigue Secondary traumatic stress experienced in caring professions. Often co-occurs with burnout in healthcare settings. "compassion fatigue" OR "secondary traumatic stress"
Moral injury Distress from actions that violate moral or professional values. Relevant in healthcare; linked to burnout and retention. "moral injury" AND (healthcare OR clinician)
Presenteeism Working while ill or impaired, reducing productivity. Functional consequence of burnout with cost implications. "presenteeism" AND (work OR productivity)
Absenteeism Missed work days due to illness, stress, or other causes. Direct outcome tied to workforce capacity and costs. "absenteeism" OR "sick leave"
Turnover intention Reported intent to leave the job within a specified period. Proxy for retention risk and intervention impact. "turnover intention" OR "intent to leave"
Recovery / Sleep Quality and quantity of sleep and off-duty recovery. Poor recovery mediates burnout risk and performance. "sleep" OR "sleep quality" OR "recovery"
