# Practitioner Evidence: Methods & Acquisition Strategy
# Document your approach to gathering expert insights and professional experience

## Practitioner Evidence Goals
**Primary Goal:** To understand how healthcare professionals experience burnout, including its key drivers, impacts on patient care, and how organizational culture contributes to or mitigates stress.
**Secondary Goal:** To gather practitioner insights on effective strategies, programs, and organizational changes that can reduce burnout and improve staff well-being in healthcare settings.

## Target Practitioners

### Industry Experts
**Target Profile:** Healthcare professionals with direct experience managing or observing employee burnout in clinical settings.
- Years of experience desired: 5-20
- Industry/sector focus: Hospitals, outpatient clinics, long-term care facilities, emergency services, behavioral health
- Role/position types: Nurse managers, physicians, charge nurses, HR/organizational development leaders in healthcare, clinical directors, healthcare administrators
- Company size experience: Medium to large healthcare systems; experience with high-volume patient environments preferred
- Geographic location: National (U.S.) or international practitioners with similar healthcare system pressures

## Acquisition Methods

### Expert Interviews
#### Interview Planning
- **Target Number:** Planning for three interviews
- **Interview Length:** 5-10 minutes
- **Interview Method:** In person and via phone
- **Recording Plan:** Audio recording and note taking

#### Interview Protocol
**Opening Questions:**
Can you tell me a bit about your role and how long you’ve been working in this healthcare setting?
How would you describe a typical day in your position?
What initially motivated you to work in healthcare, and how has your experience evolved over time?

**Problem-Focused Questions:**
What aspects of your work contribute most significantly to feelings of stress or burnout?
Can you describe a recent moment when you felt overwhelmed or emotionally drained at work?
How does burnout—your own or others’—affect patient care or team dynamics?

**Solution-Focused Questions:**
What changes or improvements would most help reduce burnout among staff in your area?
Are there any supports or strategies you’ve seen (here or elsewhere) that help prevent or lessen burnout?
What resources or tools do you think would make the biggest difference in supporting well-being?

**Implementation Questions:**
If the organization were to introduce a burnout-reduction initiative, what would be the most realistic first step?
Who would need to be involved to make these changes successful, and in what ways?
What obstacles or challenges might get in the way of implementing these solutions, and how could they be addressed?

**Closing Questions:**
Is there anything else about burnout or staff well-being that you think is important for us to understand?
What would you most want leadership to know about the experience of burnout in your role?
Would you be interested in participating in follow-up discussions or initiatives aimed at improving workplace well-being?

### Case Study Research

#### Published Case Studies
- **Target Sources:** Peer-reviewed journals: JAMA, BMJ Quality & Safety, Journals of Nursing Administration. Industry publications and Harvard business review. 
- **Search Strategy:** Use academic databases, harvard business review, review consulting firm webites for case stories.
- **Documentation Plan:** Spreadsheet format

#### Company Examples
- **Target Companies:** Cleveland Clinic (noted for robust caregiver well-being programs), Mayo Clinic (physician well-being initiatives), Yale New Haven Health (Chief Wellness Officer model)
- **Information Sources:** Research publications, linkedln posts, and workforce well-being reports 
- **Contact Strategy:** Reach out with short, respectful inquiry messages emphasizing academic purpose. Professional networks, alumni channels, and conference lists can also be used to find connections.

### Case Study Documentation
- **Template:** 
Organization & Setting
Problem Context (Burnout Indicators)
Intervention / Initiative
Implementation Steps
Outcomes & Metrics
Lessons Learned

- **Key Elements:** 
Workload factors
Interventions
Timeframe and costs
Quantifiable outcomes (burnout scores, turnover, absenteeism)
Practitioner feedback (qualitative insights)
Scalability and barriers encountered
