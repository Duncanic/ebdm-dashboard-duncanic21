# Stakeholder Evidence: Methods & Engagement Strategy

## Stakeholder Evidence Goals
**Primary Goal:** To collect stakeholder feedback on potential burnout-reduction interventions
**Secondary Goal:** To gather firsthand insights from healthcare workers and leaders about the severity, causes, and impacts of employee burnout, including workload, staffing, emotional strain, communication issues, and organizational culture.

## Target Stakeholder Groups

### Internal Stakeholders
#### Management/Leadership

- **Target Participants:** Chief Nursing Officer (CNO)
Unit Directors and Nurse Managers
Medical Director / Physician Leads
HR Leadership (HR Director, Wellbeing Program Manager)
Quality & Safety Leadership

Staffing/Scheduling Coordinators
- **Engagement Method:** Semi-structured interviews, Targeted leadership survey, Leadership focus group (optional)
- **Key Questions Focus:** How they perceive burnout levels across units, Operational barriers contributing to burnout (staffing ratios, workload, interruptions), Organizational priorities and constraints
- **Expected Response Rate:** 60–80% for interviews (leadership typically participates reliably), 50–70% for leadership surveys

#### Employees/Staff
- **Target Participants:** 
Registered Nurses (RNs)
Licensed Practical Nurses (LPNs)
Physicians and Residents
Allied Health Professionals (PT/OT, lab techs, respiratory therapists)
Support Staff (CNAs, medical assistants, clerks)
Emergency, ICU, Med-Surg, and Outpatient staff

- **Engagement Method:** 
Anonymous online survey
Voluntary focus groups (mixed or unit-specific)
One-on-one interviews for deeper insights

- **Key Questions Focus:** 
Sources of burnout (workload, staffing, emotional exhaustion, moral distress)
Impact on wellbeing, performance, and patient care
Barriers to taking breaks, using resources, or practicing self-care

- **Expected Response Rate:** 
40–60% for surveys (typical for large healthcare settings)
10–20% for focus groups (voluntary participation)

## Data Collection Methods

### Survey Design
#### Online Survey Platform
- **Platform Choice:** Qualtrics (preferred for healthcare), SurveyMonkey, or REDCap (if IRB oversight required)
- **Survey Length:** 8–12 minutes
- **Question Types:** Scale questions, open-ended questions, and multiple choice
- **Incentives:** gift cards or department recognition

#### Key Survey Questions
**Problem Assessment Questions:**
How frequently do you experience feelings of emotional exhaustion or work-related stress?
To what extent does burnout impact your daily work performance or patient care responsibilities?
How often do you feel unable to take adequate breaks or recover during your shift?
What do you see as the primary causes of burnout on your unit?

### Interview Protocol
#### Interview Structure
Duration: 30–45 minutes per interview
Format: Video conference or in-person (depending on scheduling and privacy)
Recording: With participant consent, audio-only recording using secure, encrypted tool (e.g., Teams, Zoom, or digital recorder).
Note-taking: Handwritten or typed notes during the interview; key themes summarized immediately after each session.

## Sampling Strategy

### Stakeholder Identification
#### Stakeholder Mapping

#### Sampling Approach
Survey: 100–300 staff 
Interviews: 12–20 individuals
Focus groups: 2–3 groups with 6–8 participants each

## Timeline and Logistics

### Data Collection Schedule
Survey Launch: Week 1
Survey Close: End of Week 2
Interview Period: Weeks 2–4
Focus Group Dates: Weeks 3–5
Follow-up Period: Week 6 for clarifications or additional stakeholder checks

#### Incentive Strategy
Direct Incentives: Entry into a $10–$25 gift card drawing
Indirect Benefits: Input shapes staffing decisions, workload distribution, break policies, Potential improvements in wellbeing resources
Recognition: Public acknowledgment of stakeholder participation (group-level, not individual)
